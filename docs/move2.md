# Move 2 — Hypothesis (written before coding)

## The problem I believe exists

I've experienced this myself: when I get stuck learning to build with AI, my
own explanation for why is usually wrong. I'd tell myself I needed to learn
more, or find a better tutorial — but looking back, the real issue was
almost always something behavioral, like avoiding feedback or never
finishing what I started. I didn't see that in the moment. I only saw it in
hindsight.

## My hypothesis

**If** I ask someone a mix of concrete behavior questions (projects finished
vs. abandoned, whether they've shown work publicly, how often they seek
feedback) alongside their own guess at what's blocking them,

**then** an AI can name a more accurate, more specific bottleneck than the
person's own self-diagnosis — specific enough that it's true for *them* and
false for *most* people — and predict that their current plan won't fix it.

**Because** self-report about *why* you're stuck is the least reliable part
of the data — that's the part I got wrong myself, repeatedly. But self-report
about *what you actually did* (how many projects, how often, yes/no on
public sharing) is harder to fool yourself on. The behavior questions reveal
the pattern that the self-assessment question can't, because they ask for
facts instead of explanations.

## What would prove me wrong

- If most users' self-reported blocker and the AI's diagnosis turn out to be
  the same thing — there'd be no value-add over just asking "what's wrong?"
- If users read the diagnosis, agree it sounds smart, but it doesn't change
  what they do next — the tool would be entertaining, not useful.
- If the "prediction" (what won't work) doesn't actually map to what they
  were already planning to do — then it's generic advice, not a real
  prediction.

## What I'm NOT claiming

- I'm not claiming this replaces talking to a mentor or a real community.
- I'm not claiming the 6 categories are exhaustive or scientifically
  validated — they're a useful starting taxonomy, not a diagnostic standard.
- I'm not claiming one diagnosis is enough — the follow-up/outcome loop
  exists because a single verdict without tracking what happened next is
  just a horoscope, not evidence.
